Alsace

Alsace stretches 150 km from Wissembourg on the border with German Pfalz in the north to Thann in the south.
The Vosges mountain range protects against storms from the west, so despite its northern location, Alsace is one of France's sunniest wine districts. The vineyards are located at an altitude of 200 - 400 meters, on slopes facing south and southeast. Alsace has a long history and from time to time they have belonged to Germany, they still used the typical Rhen flute bottle. And maybe thats way unlike many other areas in France, the name of the grape is on the label, such as Riesling and Gewurztraminer. In this region they almost only make one grape wines and to get a higher wine classification they are only allowed to use Riesling, Pinot gris, Gewurztraminer and muscat for white wine. And for red wine Pinot noir. For the white wine the maturation is made in steel tanks or old oak barrels so the wine do not get any taste from the barrels. This make a fresh wine with the taste of the grapes. For the red wines the maturation is made in new oak barrels take will make a wine that is full with flavour. 90% off the wine made here is white.


Speed fact

  • Green grapes:Riesling, Pinot gris, Gewurztraminer and muscat
  • Blue grapes: Pinot Noir
  • Maturation: Steel tank or old oak barrels for white wine, New oak for red wine
